Backtesting

Back-testing is a critical element in determining whether or not to deploy a particular trading strategy. Various parameters can be used to determine the suitability or otherwise of a trading strategy for deployment. Some include:

  • Ratios like Sharpe ration, Calmar ratio etc.
  • Expected profits from the trading strategy
  • Total Number of trades
  • Number of Winning/Loosing Trades
  • Average Winning Trade etc.

Buy Sell Automation

Manual trading is by a large a thing if the past. According to Wikipedia, an estimated 75% of stocks traded on US exchanges originate from automated trading system orders. The day is not far off when we touch 90 plus % figure.

The primary advantage of automating the final Buy/sell Order, even if not the entire trading strategy is that we get to place the order right at the Last Traded Price (LTP), if not the Bid-Ask price. This is particularly helpful for traders who want to minimize the bid-ask spread for illiquid stocks, in the event that the limit order is not placed.
